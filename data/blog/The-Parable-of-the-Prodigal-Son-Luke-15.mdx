## The Parable of the Prodigal Son - Luke 15

The Parable of the Prodigal Son is one of the most well-known and impactful parables of Jesus, found in Luke 15. Here’s an analysis of the parable:

### Luke 15:11-16 - The Prodigal's Departure

**Verses 11-16**: Jesus tells the story of a younger son who demands his inheritance and leaves for a distant country. He squanders his wealth in reckless living and ends up in severe need, longing to eat what the pigs were eating.

### Luke 15:17-24 - The Prodigal's Return

**Verses 17-24**: Realizing his dire situation, the son decides to return to his father and confess his sin. His father sees him from a distance, runs to him, and embraces him. The father orders a celebration, saying his son was dead and is alive again, lost and found.

### Luke 15:25-32 - The Older Brother's Reaction

**Verses 25-32**: The older brother, upon hearing the celebration, becomes angry and refuses to join. He complains to his father about the unfairness, but the father explains that they must celebrate because the lost brother has been found.

### Summary

The Parable of the Prodigal Son highlights themes of repentance, forgiveness, and the boundless love of God. It encourages believers to understand God’s joy in the return of a repentant sinner and challenges them to embrace the same attitude of forgiveness and grace.
